About
Sun Finder uses real astronomical calculations (SunCalc library) for all sunrise, sunset and azimuth values. Compass heading uses your device's orientation sensor. No data is sent to any server — everything runs locally on your device.
How calculations work
☀ Sunrise & Sunset
Uses the SunCalc astronomical library (Vladimir Agafonkin). Calculates the exact moment the sun's upper limb crosses the horizon at −0.833° altitude, accounting for atmospheric refraction. Input: your GPS coordinates + current date.
⊕ Compass Azimuth
Sun azimuth is computed from SunCalc.getPosition() which returns the sun's hour angle and declination, converted to a compass bearing (0° = North, 90° = East). Accuracy depends on your GPS precision and device magnetometer quality.
◐ Solar Panel Advisor
Optimises panel tilt (β) and azimuth (γ) by maximising day-integrated clear-sky irradiance using the cos(θ) incidence formula. Includes airmass-weighted direct beam irradiance and isotropic diffuse sky contribution. Pre-verified monthly data for Malaysia (KL). Live optimiser for all other locations. Clear-sky model only — does not account for clouds or haze.
🌐 Timezone Handling
All times displayed in your device's local timezone automatically. Solar optimiser uses a geometric UTC offset estimate (longitude ÷ 15) for day-sampling — accurate to ±30 min for most locations.
📍 Location
GPS coordinates from your device's Geolocation API. Coordinates are used only for local calculations and never transmitted to any server. Accuracy shown as the GPS error radius reported by your device.
